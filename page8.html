Exercises Reflection

Throughout the process in working on my exercise 8 for the to write CSS for background images, display types, float, height and width, overflow, and positioning was a good learning experience for me. At first, some of the properties were confusing, especially float and positioning, but experimenting with them helped me understand how they really affect a webpage. Setting background images taught me how important sizing and placement are. Changing the display property helped me fix layout issues I didn’t even realize were caused by the default settings. Float was tricky, but once I figured out how it affects surrounding elements, it made more sense. I also learned how height, width, and overflow control the space elements take up, which helped when things didn’t fit the way I expected. Positioning was one of the most challenging parts, but it gave me more control over where elements appear on the page. Overall, this activity made me more comfortable with CSS and gave me a better understanding of how each property works together to build a clean layout.
