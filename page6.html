Exercises Reflection

In Exercise 6, I created an HTML form and applied linking techniques to connect multiple webpages effectively. By working with tags such as form, input, select, textarea, fieldset, legend, and a, I learned how different input types— including text, email, date, password, and URL—collect specific kinds of user information. Using radio buttons, checkboxes, and dropdown menus further showed me how interactive elements enhance user experience and improve the clarity of digital forms. This exercise also emphasized the importance of well-structured navigation. Linking this form to previous exercise pages made the overall project more cohesive and user-friendly. Through this activity, I developed a stronger understanding of how forms act as a bridge between users and websites, allowing organized and meaningful data submission.
